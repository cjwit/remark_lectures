Indonesia * History * Dutch colony until 1949 (Dutch East Indies Company) * Java * Holds the capital of Indonesia, Jakarta * Over 100 million people * Predominantly Muslim * Bali * Never conquered by Islamic empires * Considered by many to be the cultural/artistic center * National slogan: Unity through Diversity --- # Religion in Indonesia * Islam * Muslim traders arrive in SE Asia in the 7th and 8th century * Earliest evidence of Islamic populations: 13th century * Islam is the dominant religion in Java by the 16th century * Overlaid and mixed with indigenous religion and practice * Now the largest Muslim country in the world * In Bali: Agama Tirta (Balinese Hinduism) * Only society outside India where Hinduism is the majority * Syncretism: Hinduism, Buddhism, and indigenous beliefs * Most artistic forms are from this syncretic religion --- # Religion in Indonesia * Three worlds: Upper, Middle, Lower * Upper: domain of gods and ancestors * Above Mount Agung, volcano * Brahma (creator), Wisnu (preserver), Siwa (destroyer) * Middle: Bali itself, earthly realm * Lower: realm of evil spirits * Bhutas, Leyaks are a constant threat * Begins where land meets water * Fear of battle with these other creatures * Purpose of gamelan beleganjur music --- # The Gamelan * “Ensemble”: group is one instrument * Refers to a number of different types of groups * Also refers to a set of instruments * Balinese, Javanese gamelan similarities * Instrument types * Cyclic forms * Pitch systems * Textures and organization * Association with dance and drama * Hindu elements (even in Muslim Java) * Differences from histories, values, religions --- # The Gamelan * Gamelan kebyar * Refers Meaning “lighting” * High energy, fast * Tuning system * Paired instruments, tuning * Ombak: acoustic “beats” between paired instrument * Cyclic organization * Gong cycles (see [Gamelan Mécanique site](http://digital.philharmoniedeparis.fr/gamelan/index.html)) * Melodies composed to fit these cycles --- class: middle, center .center[] ### The gong cycle --- class: middle, center .center[] ### The tuning system --- class: middle, center .center[] ### Building a melody --- class: middle, center .center[] ### Onward and upward --- class: middle, center .center[] ### Rhythmic interlocking --- class: middle, center .center[] ### Kebyar performance --- class: middle, center .center[] ### From Indonesia --- # Kecak * Reconstructs the abduction of Sita, from the Ramayana * Evil king (Rawana) kidnaps princess (Sita), rescued by Prince Rama and his army of monkeys * Musicians play roles as actors, the army of monkeys * Performed as authentic, from a 1933 film * Developed into a tourist dance/drama * Communal interdependence * Interlocking rhythmic ideas --- # Beleganjur * Gong cycle (gilak) * Gong ageng: two large gongs (male and female) * Kempur: medium size gong * Kajar: time keeping gong * Melody * Ponggang: two pair of handheld gongs (core melody) * Reyong: four “pots” (melody interlocks between them) * Drums and cymbals * Kendang: two drums (male and female), leaders * Cengceng: eight cymbal players (unison or interlocking) --- .image-100.center[![Belganjur instruments]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/beleganjur-1.png)] --- .image-100.center[![Belganjur instruments]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/beleganjur-2.png)] --- .image-100.center[![Belganjur instruments]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/beleganjur-3.png)] --- .image-100.center[![Belganjur instruments]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/beleganjur-4.png)] --- class: middle, center .center[] ### Beleganjur ensemble from the beginning of a cremation ceremony --- # Cremation rituals * Responsibility of the banjar, neighborhood organization * Sekehe gong: a local gamelan club of sorts * “Crowdedness”: Lots of music happening at once * The journey of the atma (soul) * Procession from the home to the Temple of the Dead * Interlocking nature of Lower and Middle Worlds * Battle between humans and evil spirits * Sekehe gong walks behind the tower to frightens spirits, embolden the soul, and inspires carriers * Danger of the crossroads (malpal) --- class: middle, center .center[] ### During the procession --- # Contests * Contest music (kreasi beleganjur) * Battle metaphor between humans and spirits * Historical battles * Contest battles between regional groups * Warrior image, a heroic identity * Contest style * Showy, virtuosic, costumes and choreography * Competitive, aimed to impress judges and audiences * Unlike traditional performance, which is functional --- # Contests * Similarities to traditional style * Instrumentation * Gong cycle with core ostinato and elaboration * Interlocking patterns * Differences in kreasi style * Emphasis on compositional originality * Ensemble virtuosity, showmanship, choreography * Three-part contrasting sectional forms (fast-slow-fast) * Kebyar-ization * Making beleganjur music sound more “kebyar”-ish --- class: middle, center .center[] ### Kreasi ensemble during a competition in 1992: note choreography, uniforms, tempo changes --- class: middle, center .center[] ### Kreasi ensemble during a competition in 2005: note the very difference dance steps… --- #
